Why Video Games?

Everyone has their own unique experiences with a game, and in the same way, a game can make you feel a certain way through the experience that it provides. Growing up, I didn't have the oppurtunity to go out and buy every new game that came out, or even every new system, so I came to really cherish the experiences that were afforded to me. Rather than playing games as a "one and done" kind of deal, I would play games over and over again. As I replayed games, I would always find new things in the game, put in by the creators, and I really began to appreciate the effor that was put into each game. While I wouldn't say that I am looking exclusively to code in video games, it has been a dream that I hadn't acknowledged since I was very young, and after trying it out for myself, I have grown to love it. I like to put my best effort into anything that I do because I take a lot of pride in the work that I put out. I also love being able to make an impact where I can, which was something I found through volunteering at libraries and with clubs in high school and continued in college. Making games for me is putting all of my philosophies together, and the more I learn about game development, the more I can come to appreciate games that are made as well as enjoy what I accomplish. I had a late start, but for me, it is just a beginning! I am learning a lot from my experiences making games now, and I can't wait to share the results with the rest of the world!
